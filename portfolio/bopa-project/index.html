Web App Development, Baltimore Office of Promotion & the Arts - May 2018

My team and I worked to design an app for the Baltimore Office of Promotion & the Arts (BOPA). The organization wanted a web app that would allow individuals to create and upload mural ideas from their mobile device.

We started our project by conducting a heuristic evaluation. According to the Interaction Design Foundation, a Heuristic Evaluation is “a usability inspection technique where one or a number of usability experts evaluate the user interface of a product (for example a website) against a set of Heuristic Principles (also known as Heuristics).”

Our research continued by seeking out industry examples. By doing this, we were able to find some great features to include into our application.

During our preliminary research, we created paper mockups of what we envisioned CharmMural to be. As of result of this process, we decided the application should encompass the following:

  1. A desktop version for BOPA administrators
  2. Incorporate community engagement
  3. A more minimalistic interface; less is more
  4. Streamline the building selection process

As of result of our paper prototypes, our mockups evolved and we strived to incorporate the feedback from our peers and reviewers that included the following on the app side:

  1. On Boarding Screen
  2. Seamless artist canvas
  3. Concise microcopy with helpful hints and tool guide
  4. Community gallery with ratings and geolocation for discovery

We conducted user-testing with our digital prototypes, and were able to discover the following:

  1. The user preferred certain terms over the others. Ex. Import Design/Image vs. Start from import
  2. An Option to exit the tutorial prompts
  3. Better view of the navigation in the canvas

After making these updates, we presented our app to the client.
