Website Redesign: Adopt Haiti Project - September 2019

The Adopt Haiti Project nonprofit organization enlisted my service to improve their outdated and unorganized website, while maintaining a similar look.

This is how this site looked initially:

I started by conducting a content inventory to get an understanding of what is on a website, and why it is there. During the content inventory I immediately noticed some inefficiencies.

After speaking with the president of the organization, I asked him about the main goals of the organization, and what they wanted this website to communicate. Using the information received in that conversation, with some user experience design and information architecture best practices, I developed a new IA for the website.

One of the things I noticed in my initial content inventory, is that there were multiple sections related to a festival that the organization hosts annually under various subheaders. This festival is the most important thing they do every year, so I recommended making it it’s own subheader and moving all related sections under it.

Another notable update I made to the site was updating the top image from the outdated image of the previous years festival flyer, to an image of a large group organization photo with the Haitian flag. I chose this photo because the organization president stated that the main goal of the organization was officially to “Aid Haiti by bringing Virginians and Haitians together through cultural programs, educational and informational platforms, and social events”. I felt that this image communicated that well.

The Adopt Haiti Project was very impressed with my approach and work on the website and has since asked me to continue managing and updating its contents.
