Product Testing, Nimble Pharmacy - December 2018

We conducted research to collect qualitative and quantitative data, and determine customer satisfaction with Nimble’s current digital Rx product. We then used this information to put together a presentation of proposed improvements to share directly to the client. For this project we started by considering the business need: Nimble must improve the self-serve experience so that more customers use the self service tools and fewer customers call the support line.

We evaluated usability, comprehension, and completion of actions by giving users tasks to complete on the site and monitoring the ways they do or do not complete the task.

We tested 10 participants, aged 41-55, with a variety of devices. Prior to testing, we developed our process. Testing was performed using the methods described by Steve Krug in "Don’t Make Me Think".

We used quantitative methods to compile/communicate our results.

We performed a competitive analysis to look at competitor products. This was a great way to discover what works and what does not. It was also a great way to get visual design inspiration.

The competitive analysis and results of our user tests set the stage for our recommendations, and helped validate our recommendations.

We concluded by suggesting next steps the client should take with this product.
